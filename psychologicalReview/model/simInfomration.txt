# Prediction simulations
Simulation 6: Habituated to an event in which first object moves along a straight path towards an occluded screen and the reaches the goal object. At test, it either moves through an obstacle or jumps over it.
Simulation 7: Causally effective curved motion experience during action phase prior to standard habituation (to make curved more familiar than straight)
Simulation 7b: Causally effective curved motion experience during action phase prior to standard habituation (to make curved more familiar than straight); because Simulation 7 shows that simply making the action salient, doesn't, by itself, make curved motion more familiar than straight motion, it is necessary to further boost the salience of the action phase. This is akin to causing the ball or whatever is being grasped light up in addition to the action being effective. Simulation 7b thus further boosts the salience of the action phase.
	-- note that both models make predictions; 7 predicts that all else equal, children will treat efficient and inefficient events equivalently unless the action phase's salience is further boosted, which 7b gets at
